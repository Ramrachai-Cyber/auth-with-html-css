Flag of Bangladesh - Colours, Meaning, History ?? The Bangladesh flag is a green field with a red circle slightly off-center towards the hoist. The green represents the lush greenery and youthfulness of the land, while the red circle symbolizes the sun rising over Bengal and the blood shed during the Liberation War. The flag was officially adopted on January 17, 1972. Here's a more detailed explanation: Green: The green background of the flag is symbolic of Bangladesh's lush, fertile land and the vibrancy of its youth. Red Circle: The red circle represents the rising sun, signifying the dawn of a new, independent nation after a struggle for freedom. It also symbolizes the blood of those who sacrificed their lives in the Liberation War. Positioning of the Circle: The red circle is positioned slightly off-center, towards the hoist (left side when viewed from the front), which is a key detail of the flag's design. Historical Context: The flag's design was finalized after the liberation war, with the map of Bangladesh initially present in the center being removed. This was done to differentiate it from the flag of Pakistan. Symbolism: The flag embodies the aspirations, cultural identity, and unity of the Bangladeshi people.
